Seal Entry Points

Preventing spiders from entering your home is one of the most effective preventative measures. Spiders can squeeze through incredibly small openings. Inspect the exterior of your home for cracks in the foundation, gaps around windows and doors, and openings around utility pipes and wires. Seal any identified entry points with caulk, weatherstripping, or other appropriate materials. This physical barrier makes it much harder for spiders to gain access to the interior of your property. Don't forget about smaller openings like those around dryer vents or outdoor faucets. Even tiny gaps can serve as an invitation for pests.

Why it matters: Sealing entry points directly addresses how spiders are getting into your home in the first place. Without easily accessible ways in, the number of spiders you encounter indoors will dramatically decrease. This is a fundamental step in pest exclusion and serves as a crucial line of defense. Why it matters: Clutter provides spiders with safe and secluded places to live, reproduce, and build webs.
