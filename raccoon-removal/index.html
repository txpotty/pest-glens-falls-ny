Comprehensive Raccoon Removal Services

Our raccoon removal process is designed to be thorough, safe, and effective. We don't just remove the animal; we address the root cause and potential for future problems. Our service extends beyond simple trapping and addresses the complexities of wildlife intrusion.

Initial Property Assessment

Every raccoon situation is unique. Our first step involves a detailed inspection of your property. This assessment aims to identify the extent of the infestation, pinpoint entry points, and evaluate any damage caused by the raccoons. We look for signs of activity, including tracks, droppings, nesting materials, and evidence of forced entry into areas like attics, chimneys, or crawl spaces. This comprehensive analysis is the foundation of our tailored removal plan.

Locating Entry Points

Identifying how raccoons are gaining access is paramount. We meticulously examine areas such as damaged vents, unsealed chimneys, compromised soffits, and openings in decks or sheds. Sealing these entry points is a critical part of preventing future infestations. We understand the habits of raccoons and where they are most likely to find vulnerabilities in your home's structure.

Assessing Damage

Raccoons can cause significant damage. This includes tearing up insulation for nesting material, chewing through electrical wires, damaging ductwork, and leaving behind corrosive urine and feces. Our assessment includes documenting this damage so you understand the scope of needed repairs or restoration after the animals are gone.

Humane Trapping and Removal

Our primary method for raccoon removal involves humane trapping. We use live traps specifically designed for raccoons, placed strategically based on our initial assessment. The traps are monitored regularly to ensure the animals are not left for extended periods. Once captured, the raccoons are relocated to a suitable natural habitat far from your property, adhering to local regulations and best practices for ethical wildlife management.

Trap Placement Strategy

Effective trapping requires understanding raccoon behavior. We place traps along known travel paths, near entry points, and close to evidence of nesting or feeding. Baits are carefully selected to attract raccoons specifically while minimizing the risk to non-target animals. Our technicians are trained to set traps safely and efficiently.

Relocation Protocols

The humane relocation of captured raccoons is a core principle of our service. We transport the animals to approved release sites that offer ample resources and are a safe distance from residential areas. This ensures the raccoon has a chance to thrive without posing a risk to homeowners.

Exclusion and Prevention

Removing the raccoon is only part of the solution. To prevent future intrusions, we implement exclusion strategies that seal off entry points. This might involve reinforcing damaged areas, installing chimney caps, repairing soffits, or securing crawl space vents. We use durable materials designed to withstand determined attempts by raccoons to re-enter.

Repairing Damage and Sealing Entries

After removing the animals, we can provide recommendations for or assist with repairing the damage they caused and reinforcing potential entry points. Signs You Might Need Raccoon Removal Services

Being aware of the signs of raccoon activity can help you address the problem before it escalates. Recognizing these indicators early allows for a quicker resolution and minimizes potential damage and risks. If you notice any of these signs in Glens Falls, contact us right away.

Noises in Attic or Walls

One of the most common indicators of a raccoon presence is unusual noise coming from your attic, walls, or crawl spaces, particularly at night. Raccoons are nocturnal and are often most active after dark. You might hear scratching, thumping, chattering, or even vocalizations like growling or screaming, especially if there are young raccoons present. These sounds can be unsettling and are a clear sign that a significant animal is inside your structure. Unlike smaller pests like mice or squirrels, raccoon movements are usually louder and more deliberate due to their size. Hearing these noises frequently suggests an established presence, possibly a mother and her kits, requiring professional intervention. Ignoring these sounds can lead to progressive damage and increased health risks from their waste.

Damaged Roof or Soffits

Raccoons are powerful animals with strong claws and teeth, capable of causing substantial damage to your home's exterior to gain entry. They often target weak points on roofs, like damaged shingles, fascia boards, or soffits. Look for torn-off pieces, chewed wood, or bent metal around the roofline or eaves. They may also pull apart vents or chimney caps. This damage not only provides an entry point for raccoons but can also lead to water leaks and further structural problems. Inspecting your roof regularly, especially after storms, can help identify potential vulnerabilities before raccoons exploit them. Catching this damage early is crucial for effective raccoon removal and preventing costly repairs down the line. Our team can assess this damage as part of our inspection.

Overturned Garbage Cans

Raccoons are notorious for raiding garbage cans in search of food. If you consistently find your trash cans overturned, lids removed, and garbage scattered, it's a strong indication of raccoon activity in your area. These opportunistic feeders are attracted to the smells of food waste and are persistent in their attempts to access it. securing your garbage cans with sturdy lids or placing them in a secure location overnight can help deter raccoons and confirm if they are the source of the mess. Persistent raiding despite these measures means they are likely regulars on your property and may be denning nearby. This common problem points directly towards a need for pest control services focused on larger wildlife.

Unpleasant Odors

The presence of raccoons often comes with distinct, Foul odors. Raccoon urine and feces have a strong, musky smell that can permeate your home, especially If they are denning in your attic or walls. Droppings from raccoons can also contain harmful pathogens like raccoon roundworm, making cleanup dangerous without proper precautions. Beyond the health risks, the smell itself is unpleasant and difficult to eliminate without professional cleaning and decontamination. If you notice persistent, unusual odors, particularly in upper levels of your home, it could indicate a raccoon infestation. Our removal services include recommendations or services for cleaning and sanitizing areas affected by raccoon waste, ensuring a healthy environment for your family.

Seeing Raccoons During the Day

While primarily nocturnal, seeing a raccoon out during daylight hours isn't always cause for immediate alarm. They might be foraging for food, especially if food sources are scarce, or a mother might be moving her kits. However, a raccoon acting disoriented, aggressive, or exhibiting unusual behavior during the day could be a sign of illness, such as rabies. If you encounter a raccoon acting strangely, maintain a safe distance and contact animal control or a professional wildlife removal service immediately. Never attempt to approach or interact with wildlife that appears ill or aggressive. Such sightings, particularly those indicative of potential health issues, underscore the importance of professional and safe handling of these animals.

Tracks and Droppings

Finding raccoon tracks or droppings around your property is a definitive sign of their presence. Raccoon tracks look like small handprints with five toes and are often found in mud, dirt, or snow. Their droppings, which are typically tubular and segmented, can vary in appearance depending on their diet. They often deposit droppings in communal ""latrines,"" which can be found on roofs, attics, decks, or at the base of trees. These latrines are a significant health hazard due to the potential presence of roundworm eggs. Identifying tracks and droppings helps confirm the species and can provide clues about their travel paths and denning locations. Proper identification by a professional like those at Glens Falls Pest Pros is important before attempting cleanup or removal.

Raccoons are omnivores and will readily feed on fruits, vegetables, corn, and other plants in your garden. If you find flattened garden beds, partially eaten produce, or overturned planters, raccoons could be the culprits. They are also known to dig up lawns in search of grubs and other insects, leaving behind unsightly patches of damaged turf. Pet Food Disappearance

Leaving pet food outdoors is an open invitation to raccoons and other wildlife. If you notice that pet food left outside overnight is consistently disappearing, raccoons are high on the list of potential suspects. They are attracted to the scent of pet food and will readily consume it if accessible. Bringing pet food indoors at night is a simple preventative measure. However, if you continue to have issues with raccoons or other animals being attracted to your property due to food sources, it reinforces the need for comprehensive wildlife control strategies. Raccoon Behavior and Biology in Glens Falls

Understanding the natural behavior and biology of raccoons helps explain why they interact with human environments the way they do. Recognizing their typical patterns can also aid in identifying problems and implementing effective control measures in the Glens Falls area and beyond. These fascinating mammals are highly adaptable, which is a key factor in their success in both urban and rural landscapes. Their intelligence and dexterity contribute to their ability to overcome obstacles, including human deterrents and structures. We deal with various wildlife issues, including squirrel removal and general wildlife control, and understanding species-specific traits is fundamental to our work.

Nocturnal Habits and Activity Patterns

Raccoons are primarily nocturnal animals, meaning they are most active during the night. This explains why homeowners often hear them scratching or moving in attics or walls after dark. Their nighttime activity is a strategy to avoid predators and human interaction during the day. However, as mentioned earlier, it is not uncommon to see them during the day, especially if they are a mother foraging for food for her young. Their movements often involve searching for food and exploring their territory, which can span several square miles depending on resource availability. These nocturnal habits make trapping and observing their behavior more challenging for the average homeowner.

Diet and Foraging Behavior

Raccoons are omnivores with a highly varied diet. They eat everything from fruits, nuts, and vegetables to insects, crustaceans, fish, bird eggs, and small rodents. This diverse diet makes them incredibly adaptable foragers, able to find food in almost any environment, including residential areas. They are particularly attracted to human food sources, such as garbage, pet food, and garden produce. Their clever 'hands' allow them to open containers and manipulate objects in search of food. This foraging behavior is a primary driver for their intrusion into human dwellings and why securing food sources is a critical preventative measure.

Reproduction and Denning

Raccoons typically breed in the late winter or early spring, usually having a litter of 2-5 kits in the springtime. Female raccoons seek warm, secure locations to give birth and raise their young. Attics, chimneys, sheds, and crawl spaces of homes in Glens Falls provide ideal den sites, offering protection from predators and the elements. The presence of a mother and her kits requires careful handling, as disturbing a den can be dangerous and may cause the mother to become aggressive. Our humane approach ensures the safe removal of both the mother and her young, avoiding unnecessary stress or harm to the animals. Mating seasons can increase the urgency for removal services as the problems associated with raccoons become more pronounced.

Adaptability to Urban and Suburban Environments

Raccoons have proven remarkably adaptable to living alongside humans in urban and suburban settings in Glens Falls and communities like Warrensburg. They have learned to navigate human infrastructure, utilize human-provided food sources, and find shelter in human-built structures. This adaptability has contributed to increasing conflicts between raccoons and people. Understanding their ability to thrive in these environments is crucial for developing effective long-term solutions that go beyond simple removal and include robust exclusion and prevention strategies. Managing wildlife in residential areas presents unique challenges compared to rural settings, requiring specialized knowledge and techniques.

Potential Health Risks Associated with Raccoons

Interacting with raccoons or coming into contact with their droppings poses several health risks. Raccoons are carriers of various diseases and parasites that can be transmitted to humans and pets. Rabies is a serious viral disease that can affect raccoons and is transmissible through bites or scratches. Raccoon roundworm (Baylisascaris procyonis) is another significant concern. The eggs of this parasite, found in raccoon feces, can be ingested by humans and cause severe neurological damage. Histoplasmosis, a respiratory disease, can also be contracted from inhaling fungal spores found in dried raccoon droppings. Due to these risks, it is strongly advised not to handle raccoons or their waste without proper protective equipment and training. Professional cleanup and sanitization are often necessary after a raccoon infestation to ensure a safe environment.
