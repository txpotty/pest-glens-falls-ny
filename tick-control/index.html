Additional Tips for Tick Prevention

Personal Protective Measures

In addition to professional tick control services, there are personal protective measures you can take to reduce your risk of tick bites. When spending time outdoors in tick-prone areas, wear light-colored clothing that makes it easier to spot ticks. Tuck your pants into your socks and wear long sleeves to minimize exposed skin. Use an EPA-registered insect repellent that contains DEET, picaridin, or oil of lemon eucalyptus. Always perform a thorough tick check on yourself, your children, and your pets after spending time outdoors, paying close attention to areas like the scalp, ears, armpits, groin, and back of the knees. These simple steps can significantly reduce your chances of encountering and being bitten by a tick. Combining personal prevention with professional tick control offers the most comprehensive protection against tick-borne diseases. It's a multi-layered approach that maximizes your safety and peace of mind. Protecting Your Pets from Ticks

Pets are often highly susceptible to tick bites and tick-borne illnesses. Work with your veterinarian to choose an appropriate tick prevention product for your pet, such as a topical treatment, oral medication, or tick collar. Regularly check your pets for ticks, especially after they've been in wooded or grassy areas. Pay close attention to areas around their ears, neck, and between their toes. Removing ticks promptly and correctly is essential. If you find a tick on your pet, use fine-tipped tweezers to grasp the tick as close to the skin as possible and pull upward with steady, even pressure.
